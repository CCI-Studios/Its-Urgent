The BC OAB Story

Take Action
Now

Citizens of British Columbia struggling with symptoms of overactive bladder (OAB), and their physicians, do not currently have the same access to the complete arsenal of newer medication choices that are covered by medical plans in every other province across Canada. As a result, patients utilizing B.C. PharmaCare have none of the treatment options available to other Canadians to help manage their potentially debilitating condition, which can rob patients of their dignity and independence, and have a negative impact on quality of life.

The only medication available through B.C. PharmaCare for the treatment of OAB is an older drug known to have significant side effects, including dry mouth and constipation, which are significant and well-recognized factors that may lead individuals to discontinue treatment.xxvi

Urinary incontinence is one of the symptoms of OAB. Based on The Canadian Continence Foundation Impacts of Incontinence in Canada report, urinary incontinence is cited as the "tipping point" in the decision by caregivers to place elderly family members into long term care facilities.xxvii

If the Medical Services Plan Coverage for alternative OAB medications were available to the appropriate citizens of B.C., it would not only offer renewed hope for the treatment of OAB symptoms, it will also demonstrate the province’s commitment to help some of B.C.’s most vulnerable patients, including the elderly, effectively manage a difficult condition and remain independent longer.

xxviBasra, R. et al. A review of Adherence to Drug Therapy in Patients with Overactive Bladder. BJU Int. 2008 102:774-779.

xxviiThe Canadian Continence Foundation. Impacts of Incontinence in Canada. http://www.canadiancontinence.ca/pdf/impacts-of-incontinence.pdf. Accessed May 8, 2012.
